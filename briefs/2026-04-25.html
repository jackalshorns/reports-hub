GPA Mentoring — Mike @ Clozemaster
~10:00 AM · External · Google Play Accelerator
External ▾
Key Points
  • GPA mentoring UX/UI review — Clozemaster (Mike, founder). Erin couldn't attend; Mike ran it solo.
  • Core goal: bridge intermediate learners from Duolingo to native-level fluency via high-volume fill-in-the-blank practice. Balancing gamification vs. real language progress is the central tension.
  • Onboarding friction: upfront paywall/account creation may cause drop-off before value is felt. Reverse trial (full access first, paywall later) discussed as experiment.
  • CEFR labels (A1–C2) confuse newer users unfamiliar with European proficiency standards.
  • Three metrics — word count, % mastery, level — create confusion. Level is points-based and likely needs to be removed or replaced.
  • Spaced repetition drives mastery (checkmarks per correct answer, accumulating toward collection total) — mechanic not clearly communicated to users.
  • High-priority design challenge: unify mastery, checkmarks, and spaced repetition into one teachable progression system. Breadth (# sentences) vs. depth (mastery %) framing is useful.
  • Post-round summary screens are well-hierarchized but could surface top 3 words closest to mastery with checkmark indicators.
